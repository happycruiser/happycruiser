How Middle East Conflicts Impact Cruising: What Travellers Should Know

Published: June 21, 2025

The Middle East has long been a region rich with culture, history, and stunning coastlines. However, ongoing conflicts in parts of the Middle East often raise concerns for travellers considering cruises to the region. Many families and adventure-seekers wonder: Is it safe to cruise near the Middle East? How could it affect my plans and cruise prices?

Let’s break down what’s happening and how the cruise industry is responding.

How Cruise Lines Respond to Middle East Conflicts

  • Change Itineraries: If a port or region becomes unsafe, ships will avoid it and reroute to safer destinations.
  • Cancel Excursions: Shore excursions in affected areas may be suspended or replaced.
  • Communicate Updates: Passengers are informed about itinerary changes before and during the cruise.
  • Offer Refunds or Credits: In rare cases, if a cruise is significantly changed, lines may offer partial refunds or future cruise credits.

Which Destinations Are Affected?

Popular Middle Eastern cruise ports include Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Muscat, and occasionally ports in Israel, Egypt, and Jordan. Most Gulf cruises continue as planned, especially from the UAE, which remains stable. However, areas close to active conflict zones (such as Israel, Lebanon, or the Red Sea at times) may see ships rerouted or removed from itineraries at short notice.

Should You Book a Middle East Cruise Right Now?

  • Book with Major Cruise Lines: They have strong safety protocols and the resources to respond quickly to changing situations.
  • Stay Flexible: Itineraries might change, sometimes after you’ve booked.
  • Travel Insurance is Essential: Make sure your insurance covers changes, cancellations, or evacuations related to political unrest.
  • Monitor Travel Advisories: Check Australian government travel advice (Smartraveller), cruise line updates, and reputable news.

The Upside: Cruise Lines Put Safety First

While no one can predict exactly how the situation will evolve, cruise lines have decades of experience navigating uncertain global events. Their top priority is guest and crew safety. You can trust that they’ll avoid risk and keep you informed.

For those keen to explore the Middle East’s unique ports, cruises remain one of the safest and most adaptable ways to do so.

How Middle East Conflicts Can Affect Cruise Prices

  • Discounted Fares: If demand drops for certain itineraries or ports are removed, cruise lines may lower prices to fill ships. Travellers who are flexible and willing to accept last-minute changes can sometimes score a bargain.
  • Higher Prices for Alternative Routes: When cruises are rerouted away from affected areas, remaining safe routes or itineraries (like the Mediterranean or Southeast Asia) may see an uptick in demand—and prices can rise as a result.
  • Insurance and Fuel Costs: Political instability can increase insurance and security costs for cruise lines. In some cases, this could eventually be reflected in slightly higher fares, though most lines absorb these costs to stay competitive.

Tip: If you’re considering a cruise near the Middle East, monitor prices closely. Deals can pop up as lines adjust to world events—but always prioritise safety and flexibility over savings.

Final Thoughts

The Middle East offers some truly unforgettable cruise experiences, from the dazzling skyscrapers of Dubai to ancient wonders in Jordan and Egypt. With a little research and flexibility, you can still enjoy a fantastic adventure—even in uncertain times.
